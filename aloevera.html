Aloe Vera

Aloe barbadensis miller

Aloe Vera is a succulent plant widely appreciated for its soothing gel, which has been used traditionally for skin care, minor burns, and digestive support…

Basic Information

Botanical Name: Aloe barbadensis miller

Type: Succulent

Habitat: Arid and semi-arid regions

Family: Asphodelaceae

Key Feature: Thick, fleshy leaves containing soothing gel

Common Names: Aloe, Ghritkumari

Region: Africa (native), now cultivated worldwide

1. The gel is renowned for soothing minor burns, sunburn, and skin irritation.

2. Hydrates and moisturizes skin; supports wound healing.

3. Traditionally used as a mild laxative (inner latex), though with caution.

4. Contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that may aid skin repair.

5. Used in cosmetic products for its cooling and softening properties.

Aloe Vera thrives in warm, sunny locations and well-drained sandy soil. Water sparingly, allowing the soil to dry between waterings to prevent root rot. Propagation is usually done through offsets or “pups.” Protect plants from frost; container growing is ideal for colder climates. Harvest mature outer leaves for gel after 8–12 months.
